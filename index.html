The Best Anime
of 2026, Ranked

From jaw-dropping sequels to breakout originals, these are the anime series and films that defined the year 2026 and proved that the medium has never been more vital.

Anime had a year unlike any other. With theatrical releases smashing global box office records and streaming platforms locked in an arms race for the best simulcasts, the medium entered 2026 with more momentum than it has ever had. Here, we rank the best of the best — the shows and films that weren't just good anime, but great storytelling by any measure.

Whether you're a lapsed fan or have never missed an episode of anything, this list is your guide. We've watched everything so you don't have to — but you really, really should.

The Rankings
1
Action · Shonen

Demon Slayer: Infinity Castle Arc

Ufotable · Crunchyroll · 2026
Replace with your image

After years of buildup, Ufotable delivered something that can only be described as a generational event. The Infinity Castle arc takes everything the series has been building — the grief, the brotherhood, the spectacularly rendered fire and water effects — and cranks it to an intensity that makes the Mugen Train feel like a warmup. Tanjiro's final clash is one of the most beautifully animated sequences in the history of the medium, full stop.

Watch on Crunchyroll
2
Dark Fantasy · Psychological

Chainsaw Man Season 2

MAPPA · Prime Video · 2026
Replace with your image

The second season of Chainsaw Man is a masterclass in tonal control. Where the first season established MAPPA's experimental visual language, Season 2 weaponizes it. The Chainsaw Man Church arc is structurally unlike almost anything else on TV — a horror story that doubles as a satire of fandom itself, rendered in animation that swings from classical beauty to deliberate grotesquerie with complete confidence.

Watch on Prime Video

3
Sci-Fi · Thriller

Sakamoto Days

TMS Entertainment · Netflix · 2026
Replace with your image

An ex-assassin who just wants to run a convenience store shouldn't be this exhilarating. Netflix's adaptation of Yuto Suzuki's manga turned out to be one of the year's most pleasant surprises — a series that understands comedy and action aren't opposing forces but symbiotic ones. The choreography alone earns it a place on this list.

Watch on Netflix
4
Fantasy · Adventure

Frieren: Beyond Journey's End — Cour 3

Madhouse · Crunchyroll · 2026
Replace with your image

Frieren has always been a series about time — and how different it feels depending on who you are. The third cour deepens that meditation considerably, introducing a narrative strand about Frieren's own past that recontextualizes everything that's come before. It remains the most emotionally sophisticated thing airing anywhere.

Watch on Crunchyroll
5
Thriller · Crime

Monster (Remake)

Wit Studio · HBO Max · 2026
Replace with your image

Wit Studio's reimagining of Naoki Urasawa's seminal manga is a triumph of restraint. Where the original Madhouse adaptation was beloved but meandering, this version runs at a tighter pace without sacrificing the moral complexity that made Johan Liebert one of fiction's great villains. The collaboration with HBO results in production values that match the story's ambitions completely.

Watch on Max
6
Romance · Slice of Life

Dungeon Meshi Season 2

Trigger · Netflix · 2026
Replace with your image

Delicious in Dungeon's second season picks up exactly where the first left off — deeper in the dungeon, stranger in its cooking sequences, and more emotionally resonant than ever. Trigger has fully inhabited Ryoko Kui's world, and the result is a show that makes you feel the warmth of a shared meal and the terror of a dragon all within the same episode.

Watch on Netflix
7
Mecha · Drama

Gundam: Requiem for Vengeance

Sunrise · Netflix · 2026
Replace with your image

This CG-animated Gundam film is the franchise's most successful integration of three-dimensional rendering, trading the uncanny valley of earlier attempts for something genuinely cinematic. Set during the One Year War and told from a purely ground-level perspective, Requiem for Vengeance is a war film that happens to have giant robots — and is better for the distinction.

Watch on Netflix
